    Psychology Materials

    On this website you'll find programming resources and other research related help gathered and sometimes created by me. If you're looking to find the programming resources, great! But, I have a big warning and caution for you. I am not a programmer. In fact I took one computer science class in college as a senior and got a B+. I picked up "functional" programming in graduate school mostly thanks to David Warren who actually is both very smart and very skilled. Functional to me means it works. Does it look pretty? No! (pleases don't view source on these pages). Is it optimized? No! Does it follow best practices? No! I'm not bragging about its faults, but rather trying to be as upfront about the products here as possible (taking a page from the MIT license). So that is why everything here is released open source. Take my work, remix it, make it better, but please keep it open source, and if I have these copylefts correct, don't try to make money off of it. Yeah, there is a CC at the bottom, but if you do or don't cite me, no big deal.

    Purpose

    The purpose of these resources is to educate and help people. You'll find information to help with Statistics, Writing in Science, and experiments for Introductory Psychology, and Cognition, Learning and Memory. You'll also find programs related to my own research and other random projects.
